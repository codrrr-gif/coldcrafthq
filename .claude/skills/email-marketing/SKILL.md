## Core Operating Principles

### 1. Relevance Is Infrastructure, Not Nicety
The same filters that protect sender reputation also maximize reply rates. Micro-segmented
campaigns to tight, intent-matched lists outperform broad sends on every dimension: open
rate, reply rate, positive reply rate, inbox placement, and domain health. Segmentation is
not a personalization feature — it is the operating foundation of the whole system.

### 2. The 30/30/50 Rule
30% of results come from copy quality. 30% from list targeting precision. 50% from follow-up
strategy and persistence. Most operators optimize the 30% (copy) and neglect the 50% (follow-up
architecture). Elite operators build the full system.

### 3. Positive Reply Rate Beats Raw Reply Rate
A 10% reply rate with 30% positive replies produces fewer meetings than a 6% reply rate
with 70% positive replies. Always optimize for positive reply rate and meetings per 1,000
sent — not open rates or total reply rates. Open rates are increasingly unreliable (Apple MPP
distortion). Meetings are the only metric that pays.

### 4. The System Must Handle What It Generates
An outbound system that generates replies faster than they can be handled is broken. Unibox
operations, AI Reply Agent configuration, subsequence triggers, and CRM handoff are not
afterthoughts — they are part of the system architecture that must be designed before launch.

### 5. Test One Variable At A Time, Ship Winners Fast
The fastest path to elite performance is disciplined A/Z testing at every layer: ICP, hook
type, subject line, opening line, CTA, send window, sequence length. One test per variable.
Ship winners, retire losers, move to the next variable. This is how the top quartile (15%+
reply rates) separates from the average (3-5%).

### 6. Instantly Handles The Infrastructure. You Handle The Strategy.
Instantly's warmup network (4.2M+ accounts), inbox rotation, automated Inbox Placement tests,
bounce auto-pause, and domain health monitoring handle the technical deliverability layer.
Your job is to build the strategy on top of that foundation: list quality, segmentation,
sequence design, copy direction, testing discipline, and reply conversion.

## Diagnostic Protocol — Run Before Every Campaign

Before building any campaign, answer all of these. Missing answers = missing results.

**ICP Clarity**:
- Who specifically is the ideal respondent? (Title, seniority, company type, size, industry)
- What is their most acute pain point right now — not in general, but *right now*?
- What buying signal or trigger makes this person *in-market* vs. just a fit?
- What are their competitive alternatives? What are those alternatives' weaknesses?

**List Quality**:
- Where is this list sourced? Is it verified? What is the expected bounce rate?
- Is the list micro-segmented or is it a broad ICP dump?
- Is there overlap with previous campaigns? (Dedup before import)
- Does the personalization data (company name, role, trigger) actually exist for each contact?

**Sequence Architecture**:
- How many steps? What is the day spacing? (Default: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7)
- Does each step bring a new angle, new proof, or new value — or just follow up?
- What subsequences are triggered by positive/neutral/OOO replies?
- What is the CTA? Is it the lowest-friction ask possible for a cold prospect?

**Copy Direction** (hand off to Elite Copywriting skill):
- What is the prospect's awareness level? (Almost always: Problem Aware or Solution Aware)
- What hook type will lead the sequence? (Problem, Timeline, Social Proof, Curiosity)
- What is the single desired action? (Reply, book call, confirm interest)
- Read `references/copy-integration.md` before writing or briefing copy

**Testing Plan**:
- What two variants will be A/Z tested first? (Subject line is highest-leverage first test)
- What is the minimum send threshold before declaring a winner? (150-200 per variant)
- What metric determines the winner? (Positive reply rate, not just open rate)

**Reply Infrastructure**:
- Is Unibox configured with correct AI Reply Labels?
- Is AI Reply Agent in HITL mode (recommended until 20-30 approved responses)?
- What CRM does a positive reply trigger? Is the integration live and tested?
- What is the SLA for responding to a positive reply? (Target: under 1 hour)

## The Performance Benchmarks

Know where you stand before you optimize:

| Metric | Below Average | Solid | Excellent | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reply Rate** | <2% | 5-10% | 10-15% | 15%+ |
| **Positive Reply Rate** | <30% of replies | 40-50% | 50-65% | 65%+ |
| **Open Rate** (directional only) | <25% | 40-55% | 55-70% | 70%+ |
| **Hard Bounce Rate** | >2% | <2% | <1% | <0.5% |
| **Inbox Placement** | <60% primary | 70-80% primary | 80-90% | 90%+ primary |
| **Meetings per 1,000 sent** | <3 | 5-10 | 10-20 | 20+ |

**The true north metric**: Meetings per 1,000 emails sent (or cost per meeting). Everything
else is a diagnostic input to improve this number.
