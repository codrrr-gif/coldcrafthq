## The Core Principle

Scripts are not rigid word-for-word formulas. They're FRAMEWORKS — proven
sequences of psychological steps that guide a prospect from attention to
purchase. The personality, stories, and details change with every product.
The STRUCTURE stays the same because human psychology stays the same.

## Script Selection Decision Matrix

Use this matrix to select the right script for any funnel element:

| Situation | Price Point | Script | Typical Length | Close Rate Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lead magnet / free offer landing page | $0 (free + shipping) | Who/What/Why/How | 2-3 min video | 20-40% opt-in |
| Upsell immediately after any purchase | $27-$297 | OTO Script | 3-5 min video | 15-25% |
| Mid-range product, cold/warm traffic | $27-$997 | Star, Story, Solution | 15-60 min / 2K-10K words | 1-5% cold, 5-15% warm |
| Mid-to-high ticket, list or webinar traffic | $297-$2,997 | Perfect Webinar | 60-90 min | 10-20%+ with Stack |
| New product launch to existing audience | $297-$1,997 | Product Launch (4 videos) | 4×10-20 min over 7-10 days | 5-15% of engaged viewers |
| Invisible funnel registration | $297-$997 | Magic Bullet | 2-3 min | ~85% show-up, ~90% pay |
| High-ticket coaching/consulting | $5,000-$100K+ | Two-Step Phone Close | 75-120 min (2 calls) | 20-40% of qualified apps |
| Order form bump (any funnel) | $17-$97 | Single-paragraph bump copy | 2-3 sentences | 30-40% |
| Downsell after declined upsell | 50-70% of OTO price | Modified OTO (empathy open) | 1-2 min video | 10-20% |

**Decision logic:**
1. What Value Ladder rung is this offer on? → Determines price range
2. What's the traffic temperature? → Cold needs longer scripts, hot needs shorter
3. Is this the initial offer or an upsell? → Initial = full script, upsell = OTO script
4. What medium? → Video = tighter script, text = more detail, phone = conversational

## Script #1: Who, What, Why, How

**Best for:** Low-cost offers (free + shipping, trials, lead magnets)
**Length:** 2-3 minutes (video) or equivalent short copy
**Used in:** Two-Step Funnel, Continuity trial, webinar registration

> **Cross-ref:** For the funnel architecture this script lives inside, see `funnel-types.md` Section 2 (Two-Step Free + Shipping).

**The structure:**

**WHO** — Introduce yourself briefly.
"Hi, I'm [Name], founder of [Company]."

**WHAT** — State what you have.
"I've got a free [product] that's going to show you [result]."

**WHY** — Explain why they need it.
"If you've been struggling with [problem], you need this because [benefit]."

**HOW** — Tell them how to get it.
"Just fill out the form on this page with your shipping address, and I'll
send it out right away."

**THE CATCH** — Address the "too good to be true" objection.
"There's no catch. I'm doing this because [reason]. All I ask is you help
cover shipping."

**URGENCY** — Why they should act now.
"I only have [X] copies left / This offer ends [date]."

**GUARANTEE** — Remove risk.
"If you don't love it, I'll refund your money and you keep the [product]."

**RECAP** — Remind them what they're getting.
"One more time, here's what you'll receive: [list]."

### Who/What/Why/How — Fill-in-the-Blank Template

```
[VIDEO OPENS — direct to camera, casual setting]

WHO:
"Hey, I'm [FULL NAME], [CREDIBILITY MARKER — e.g., founder of X /
I've helped Y people do Z]."

WHAT:
"I just put together a [FORMAT: book/guide/toolkit/checklist] called
[PRODUCT NAME], and I want to send you a free copy."

"Inside, you'll discover:
  • [SPECIFIC OUTCOME #1 — most compelling]
  • [SPECIFIC OUTCOME #2 — addresses common pain]
  • [SPECIFIC OUTCOME #3 — unexpected benefit]"

WHY:
"Look, if you've been [PAIN STATE — struggling with X / frustrated by Y /
stuck at Z], this is going to change everything because [MECHANISM —
the specific reason WHY this works differently]."

HOW:
"All you have to do is [EXACT ACTION — fill out the form below / click
the button / enter your shipping address], and I'll [DELIVERY METHOD —
ship it to your door / send it to your inbox / give you instant access]."

THE CATCH:
"Now you might be wondering — what's the catch? There is no catch.
I'm doing this because [REAL REASON — e.g., I want to get this into
as many hands as possible / every person who reads this becomes a
customer for life]. All I ask is you cover [SMALL ASK — $7.95 shipping /
your email address]."

URGENCY:
"Fair warning: [REAL CONSTRAINT — I only printed 5,000 copies / this
page comes down on Friday / I'm testing this offer and it may not last]."

GUARANTEE:
"And if you [USE THE PRODUCT] and don't [GET THE RESULT], just
[REFUND MECHANISM — email us / click one link] and I'll [GUARANTEE —
refund every penny, and you keep the book]."

RECAP:
"So one more time — you're getting [PRODUCT] that shows you how to
[BIG RESULT], plus [BONUS IF ANY], for just [PRICE/FREE].
[FINAL CTA — Click the button below and fill out the form.]"
```

## Script #2: OTO (One-Time Offer)

**Best for:** Upsell pages immediately after a purchase
**Length:** 3-5 minutes (video)
**Used in:** Every funnel — after any initial purchase

> **Cross-ref:** OTO conversion rate targets and placement strategy in `funnel-types.md` Funnel Economics table. For stacking multiple OTOs with downsells, see `value-ladder.md` Section 3 (Money Model).

**The structure:**

**CONFIRM INITIAL DECISION**
"Wait — your order is not yet complete."
(NOT "Congratulations, your order is complete" — that closes the buying loop)

**SMART → WHY**
"You've made a smart choice, and here's why... You ordered this because
you wanted [result], and that's exactly what it's going to do."

**3X 2X QUESTION**
"How would you like to get [3x the results] in [half the time]?"

**EXCLUSIVE**
"This offer is NOT for everyone. We're only making it available because
you proved you're an action taker."

**RESULTS, FAST, SPEED**
"What I'm about to share will help you get [result] in half the time."

**THE ONE THING**
Don't explain everything about the upsell product. Focus on ONE specific
thing inside it that delivers the most value. Explain what it is, how it works,
and what results it produces.

**FUTURE PACING**
"Can you imagine what your life will be like when you have [the one thing]?"

**CTA**
"Click the button below to add [product] to your order."

**GUARANTEE**
Reverse the risk.

**VALUE STACKING**
Add bonuses. Pull the most desired element out and offer it as a "free bonus."

**SCARCITY**
"This is available on our website for [higher price]. Right now, you have
ONE chance to get it for [lower price]. When you leave this page, it's gone."

**SECOND CTA**
Repeat the call to action.

**TESTIMONIAL RUSH**
Stack as many testimonials as possible at the end.

### OTO Script — Critical Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Kills Conversion | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| "Congratulations, your order is complete!" | Closes the buying loop — brain shifts to "done" mode | "Wait — your order is not yet complete" |
| Explaining every feature of the upsell | Overwhelms; buyer's decision fatigue is high post-purchase | Focus on THE ONE THING — single most valuable element |
| No connection to initial purchase | Feels like a random pitch, breaks trust | Explicitly link: "Because you just got X, you need Y to..." |
| Same price or higher than initial purchase | Sticker shock after they just spent | OTO #1 should be 1-2x the frontend price, max |
| No downsell on decline | Lose 100% of decliners | Always offer a downsell: smaller version, payment plan, or different angle |

### Downsell Script Variation

When a buyer clicks "No thanks" on an OTO, the downsell fires:

```
"Wait — I completely understand. [PRODUCT] isn't for everyone.

But before you go, I want to make sure you don't miss out on
[THE ONE THING from the OTO — the single most valuable piece].

I've pulled out just [COMPONENT] and I'm offering it separately
for only [50-70% of OTO PRICE].

[If payment plan:] Or if you'd prefer, you can split it into
[X] easy payments of [Y].

This way you still get [CORE BENEFIT] without [the full commitment].

[CTA — Yes, add this to my order for just $X]"
```

## Script #3: Star, Story, Solution

**Best for:** Mid-range products ($27-$997)
**Length:** 15-60 minutes (video) or 2,000-10,000 words (text)
**Used in:** Self-Liquidating Offer Funnel

> **Cross-ref:** For the SLO funnel architecture, see `funnel-types.md` Section 3. For the psychology behind each step (pattern interrupt, social proof, future pacing), see Elite Copywriting → `psychology.md`. For long-form sales page execution, see Elite Copywriting → `sales-pages.md`.

This is the workhorse sales script. 44 steps organized into three sections.
Each step can be a single sentence or several paragraphs.

### Section 1: STAR

1. **Pattern Interrupt** — Grab attention. Break their mental state.
2. **Core-Desire Questions** — Questions that make them think about what they want.
3. **Agitate Past Failures** — "This isn't the first time you've tried, is it?"
4. **Big Promise / The One Thing** — "When you [watch/read] this, you'll discover [X]."
5. **Introduce the Star** — Your Attractive Character enters.

### Section 2: STORY

6. **High Drama** — Start at the most dramatic moment (not the beginning).
7. **Backstory → WALL** — How you got to that moment. You hit a wall.
8. **Identify the Problem** — What specifically was wrong.
9. **Epiphany / Declaration** — The moment everything changed.
10. **Path to Solution** — Things you tried along the way.
11. **First Sign of Success** — It started working.
12. **Conspiracy** — The cards were stacked against you.
13. **The Big Lie** — What "they" told you that wasn't true.
14. **Common Enemy** — Who/what was really to blame.
15. **Rapid Growth** — Once you knew the truth, things took off.
16. **Case Studies** — Others who've had the same success.
17. **Hidden Benefits** — Unexpected positive outcomes.

### Section 3: SOLUTION

18. **Formal Introduction** — "That's why I created [Product]."
19. **Pain and Cost** — What it took to create this.
20. **Ease** — How much easier it makes things.
21. **Speed** — How much faster it makes things.
22. **"So" Benefits** — "[Feature], so [benefit to them]."
23. **Social Proof** — Testimonials.
24. **Make the Offer** — "Would you like to get access to [X]?"
25. **Build Value** — Add bonuses (must support The One Thing).
26. **Float a Fake Price** — "The total value of all this is [high number]."
27. **Emotional Close (If/All)** — "If all this did was [outcome], would it be worth it?"
28. **Reveal Real Price** — "I'm not going to charge you [high]. Only [actual price]."
29. **Guarantee** — Risk reversal with a creative name.
30. **Inject Scarcity** — Legitimate reason to act now.
31. **Future Pacing** — "Imagine what life will be like when..."
32. **Call to Action** — Exactly what to click and what happens next.
33. **Post Selling** — "For those already signing up, here's what happens next..."
34. **Take Away Selling** — "It doesn't matter to us if you sign up or not."
35. **Close with Reminder** — Recap the full offer for skimmers.

### Star, Story, Solution — Pacing Guide

| Section | % of Total Length | Purpose | Energy Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| STAR (Steps 1-5) | 10-15% | Hook and qualify | High — grab attention fast |
| STORY (Steps 6-17) | 40-50% | Build belief and desire | Rising — emotional journey |
| SOLUTION (Steps 18-35) | 35-45% | Present and close | Peak → measured close |

**Common mistake:** Rushing the STORY section. If the story doesn't land, no amount of offer stacking will save the close. The story is where belief transfer happens. Spend 40-50% of your total length here.

## Script #4: The Perfect Webinar

**Best for:** Mid-to-high-ticket offers ($297-$2,997)
**Length:** 60-90 minutes
**Used in:** Perfect Webinar Funnel (live or automated)

> **Cross-ref:** For the webinar funnel architecture (registration → confirmation → live room → replay → close), see `funnel-types.md` Section 5 including Evergreen Webinar Deep Dive. For the Attractive Character identity that delivers the webinar, see `communication-funnels.md` Part 1.

### Introduction (5 minutes)

- **Credibility Statement** — Who you are and why you're qualified
- **Big Promise** — "In the next 60 minutes, you'll learn how to [X] without [Y]"
  (This is the ruler they'll judge your webinar by — set it deliberately)
- **Hook to End** — Give them a reason to stay to the end (giveaway, bonus, secret link)
- **Command Attention** — "Close Facebook. Turn off your phone. Give me your full attention."
- **Qualify Yourself** — Brief backstory establishing credibility
- **Future Pace** — "Imagine what your life will be like when you know how to [X]..."

### Content: The Three Secrets (50-60 minutes)

**The One Thing:** Frame your entire teaching as:
"How to [result] without [pain/obstacle]"

**Three Secrets:** Each secret breaks one LIMITING BELIEF that would
prevent the purchase. Structure:
- Belief the audience holds (that's wrong)
- New belief you install (that's true)
- Evidence/story/proof supporting the new belief

**Secret #1:** Breaks the belief about the VEHICLE (why this type of solution works)
**Secret #2:** Breaks the belief about INTERNAL capability (why THEY can do it)
**Secret #3:** Breaks the belief about EXTERNAL obstacles (why now is the time)

### The Three Limiting Beliefs — Deep Dive

This is the most critical framework in the Perfect Webinar. Every objection a
prospect has falls into one of three categories. Your three secrets must
systematically destroy all three:

**Belief Category 1: VEHICLE (External — "This won't work")**

The prospect doesn't believe this TYPE of solution can deliver the result.
They've been burned before by similar-sounding promises.

- What they think: "I've tried [category] before and it didn't work"
- What you must prove: This is a NEW VEHICLE — fundamentally different from
  what they've tried. Not an improvement on the old thing. A NEW thing.
- How to prove it: Epiphany Bridge story — tell the story of how YOU discovered
  this vehicle and had the "aha" moment that changed everything.
- Example: "You don't need another diet. You need to understand how your
  hormones control fat storage — that's why nothing else worked."

**Key principle:** You are NOT selling an improvement. You are selling a NEW
OPPORTUNITY. Improvements trigger comparison and skepticism. New opportunities
trigger curiosity and hope. (Expert Secrets, Ch. 3)

**Belief Category 2: INTERNAL ("I can't do it")**

The prospect believes the vehicle might work but doubts their own ability
to execute it. Self-doubt, imposter syndrome, past failures.

- What they think: "Maybe it works, but I'm not smart/experienced/disciplined enough"
- What you must prove: They already have everything they need. The system
  does the heavy lifting. Others in their exact situation succeeded.
- How to prove it: Tell the story of your WORST case study — the person who
  had every disadvantage and still succeeded. If they can do it, anyone can.
- Example: "Sarah was a single mom working two jobs with zero tech experience.
  She followed the system for 90 days and replaced her income."

**Belief Category 3: EXTERNAL ("Now isn't the right time")**

The prospect believes in the vehicle AND themselves but thinks external
circumstances prevent them from acting. Time, money, market conditions,
spouse's approval, economy.

- What they think: "I need to wait until [condition] before I can start"
- What you must prove: Waiting is the REAL risk. The external obstacle is
  either solvable or irrelevant. The cost of inaction exceeds the cost of action.
- How to prove it: Show the math of delay. What does another 6 months of
  the current situation COST them? Then show how the program accounts for
  the external obstacle.
- Example: "You think you don't have time? You're spending 3 hours a day
  commuting to a job you hate. This system takes 1 hour a day. In 90 days
  you won't need the commute at all."

### The Epiphany Bridge

The Epiphany Bridge is the storytelling structure used within each Secret.
It bridges the prospect from their current (wrong) belief to your new
(correct) belief through a story — not through logic or data.

**Structure:**

```
1. THE BACKSTORY
   "I used to believe [OLD BELIEF — the same thing they believe now]..."

2. THE WALL
   "I was [doing everything right by conventional wisdom] but
   [still getting bad results]..."

3. THE EPIPHANY
   "Then one day, I [discovered/realized/stumbled onto] [NEW BELIEF]..."
   (This is the emotional "aha" moment — make it vivid and specific)

4. THE FRAMEWORK
   "Here's what I learned: [Teach the new belief as a simple framework]..."

5. THE ACHIEVEMENT
   "Once I understood this, [specific results with numbers]..."

6. THE TRANSFORMATION
   "And it wasn't just me — [case study name] did the same thing and
   [their specific result]..."
```

**Why stories, not logic:** People don't buy based on information. They buy
based on the FEELING of an epiphany. Your job is to recreate the epiphany
you had — the emotional moment of "everything just clicked" — inside the
prospect's mind. Logic creates understanding. Epiphany creates ACTION.

**One Epiphany Bridge per Secret.** Three Secrets = Three Epiphany Bridges =
Three belief shifts. By the end of your content section, the prospect believes:
1. This vehicle works (and is different from what they've tried)
2. They can do it (even with their specific limitations)
3. Now is the time (waiting costs more than acting)

With all three beliefs in place, the only remaining question is "how" — and
that's what your offer answers.

### The Stack (10-20 minutes)

**Transition:** "Let me ask you a question..."

**Present each component of the offer individually:**
- Introduce Component 1 → Show Stack Slide with Component 1
- Introduce Component 2 → Show Stack Slide with Components 1 + 2
- Introduce Component 3 → Show Stack Slide with Components 1 + 2 + 3
- Continue until all components are shown
- Final Stack Slide shows EVERYTHING together

### Stack Slide Design — The Close Rate Multiplier

The Stack Slide is the single highest-leverage element of the webinar close.
Brunson credits this technique with taking his close rates from 5-10% to 20%+.

**How to build the Stack Slide:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          EVERYTHING YOU GET TODAY            │
│                                             │
│  ✓ [CORE OFFER NAME]                 $X,XXX│
│  ✓ [BONUS #1 NAME]                     $XXX│
│  ✓ [BONUS #2 NAME]                     $XXX│
│  ✓ [BONUS #3 NAME]                     $XXX│
│  ✓ [BONUS #4 NAME]                     $XXX│
│  ✓ [FAST-ACTION BONUS]                 $XXX│
│                                             │
│  TOTAL VALUE:                       $XX,XXX│
│                                             │
│  YOUR INVESTMENT TODAY:                     │
│          >>>  $X,XXX  <<<                   │
│                                             │
│  [GUARANTEE STATEMENT]                      │
│                                             │
│  [CTA BUTTON]                               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Stack Slide rules:**
1. Each component must have its own value assigned (anchor high but credibly)
2. Re-show the FULL stack with every new addition — don't just show the new item
3. The total value should be 10-20x the actual price (creates perceived value gap)
4. The last thing they see before price reveal is the FULL stack — maximum perceived value
5. Bonuses should address the three limiting beliefs:
   - Bonus for vehicle belief: "Quick Start Guide" (proves the system is simple)
   - Bonus for internal belief: "Done-For-You Templates" (removes need for skill)
   - Bonus for external belief: "Private Community Access" (provides support/accountability)

**Offer component selection:**

| Component Type | Purpose | Pricing Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Core Training/Product | The main thing they're buying | 60-70% of total stated value |
| Tool/Template/Swipe File | Removes "I can't do it" belief | $200-$500 each |
| Community/Group Access | Removes "I'm alone" fear | $500-$2,000 |
| Live Coaching Calls | Removes "what if I get stuck" fear | $1,000-$5,000 |
| Fast-Action Bonus | Rewards immediate decision | $200-$500 |

### The Close Sequence

**If/All Close:** "If all this did was [specific outcome], would it be worth [price]?"
(Use both toward-pleasure and away-from-pain versions)

**Reveal Price:** Show total value → "I'm not going to charge you [total value].
Your investment today is only [actual price]."

**Close:** Guarantee + Scarcity + Final CTA

**The If/All Close — Worked Example:**

```
"If all this program did was help you land your first three clients
in the next 30 days — clients paying you $2,000 each — would it
be worth [price]? [PAUSE]

And if all it did was give you a system so you never had to wonder
where your next client is coming from... would that be worth it?

And what if all it did was free you from your 9-to-5 within the
next 12 months — would that ALONE be worth [price]?

Well, it does ALL of that. And more."
```

**Post-close urgency stack:**
1. Fast-action bonus (disappears after webinar ends)
2. Limited enrollment (real capacity constraint)
3. Price increase (going up after this cohort)
4. Guarantee with teeth (specific, named, time-bounded)

## Script #5: Product Launch Sequence

**Best for:** New product releases ($297-$1,997)
**Length:** 4 videos, 10-20 minutes each, released over 7-10 days
**Used in:** Product Launch Funnel

> **Cross-ref:** For the Product Launch funnel page flow, see `funnel-types.md` Section 7. For the email sequence that drives viewers between videos, see `communication-funnels.md` (Soap Opera structure adapted for launches).

**Video 1: "Wow and How"**
- Big Promise (The One Thing)
- Kill myths and objections
- Show others achieving this result (case studies)
- Hook to Video 2

**Video 2: "Transformational Education"**
- "Let's do it together — I'll show you how"
- Walk through the process step by step (overview, not full detail)
- Show proof it works (your results + student results)
- Hook to Video 3

**Video 3: "Ownership Experience"**
- Future pace: "Imagine what life looks like when..."
- Walk them through doing it themselves (in their imagination)
- Hint that you have something that makes it even easier
- Hook to Video 4

**Video 4: "The Offer"**
- "I created [Product] because I want you to succeed"
- What you get / How it works / What it does for you
- How to order / What happens next

### Launch Sequence — Belief Mapping

Each video in the sequence maps to the same three-belief framework as the
Perfect Webinar, spread across multiple days:

| Video | Primary Belief Addressed | Psychological Objective |
|---|---|---|
| Video 1: Wow and How | Vehicle belief | "This is a new opportunity worth my attention" |
| Video 2: Education | Internal belief | "I could actually do this" |
| Video 3: Ownership | External belief | "Now is the time — I can see myself succeeding" |
| Video 4: The Offer | All three (reinforcement) | "Here's how to get started" |

**Between-video emails (sent day after each video):**
- Recap key insight from previous video
- Open loop for next video
- Include 1-2 testimonials that reinforce the belief addressed
- Soft CTA: "Reply and tell me your biggest takeaway"

**Launch scarcity timeline:**
- Days 1-3: Video 1 live, Video 2 teased
- Days 4-5: Video 2 live, Video 3 teased
- Days 6-7: Video 3 live, cart open teased
- Day 8: Video 4 (cart open), fast-action bonus
- Day 9-10: Cart open, urgency emails (48-hour countdown)
- Day 10: Cart closes (hard deadline)

## Script #7: High-Ticket Phone Close (Two-Step)

**Best for:** $5,000-$100,000+ coaching/consulting
**Length:** 75-120 minutes (total across both calls)

> **Cross-ref:** For the High-Ticket Application funnel flow (ad → application → set call → close call), see `funnel-types.md` Section 8. For the application page copy, see Elite Copywriting → `sales-pages.md`.

### The Set Call (Setter)

**Introduction:** Natural, low-key conversation
**Questions:** Find where they ARE and where they WANT TO BE
- What are their goals?
- WHY do they want that? (Dig for emotions — this is the real reason they'll buy)
- What's holding them back? (Usually "I don't know how")

**Pivotal Question:** "If you knew how to [achieve goal] in [timeframe], would you do it?"
(They say yes — they've just sold themselves)

**Blast:** Brief taste of what the program offers
**Self-Sell Question:** "If you could work with [Expert], do you think you'd succeed? Why?"
(LET THEM TALK. Their reasons are more convincing than yours.)

**Posture:** "I'm not the expert — I find qualified people. The Program Director
will decide if you're a fit."

### The Close Call (Closer)

**Magnify the Pain:** Revisit what the setter uncovered
**Present the Solution:** This is how we help people in your exact situation
**Handle Objections:** Using the emotional reasons THEY gave the setter
**Close:** Investment, terms, next steps

### High-Ticket Close — Objection Handling Framework

The four objections every closer will face, and the framework for each:

| Objection | What They're Really Saying | Response Framework |
|---|---|---|
| "I need to think about it" | Fear of making the wrong decision | "What specifically do you need to think about? Let's think through it together right now." Then isolate: is it the money, the fit, or the timing? |
| "I can't afford it" | Haven't connected investment to ROI | "I hear you. Can I ask — what's the cost of NOT solving this? What will the next 12 months look like if nothing changes?" Then: payment plan option. |
| "I need to talk to my spouse" | Need permission / shared financial decision | "Totally understand. What do you think they'll say? ... What if they say no?" Pre-handle: "Would it help if we got them on a quick call?" |
| "The timing isn't right" | External belief not broken | "When WOULD be the right time? ... What will be different then? ... The truth is, the timing will never feel perfect. The best time is when you have access to support." |

**Set → Close handoff protocol:**
1. Setter records: goals, emotional WHY, pain points, objections surfaced, budget signals
2. Closer reviews notes BEFORE the call (never go in cold)
3. Closer opens with: "I spoke with [Setter], and they told me [specific thing about the prospect]" — proves this is personalized, not a script
4. Closer uses the prospect's OWN WORDS from the set call to close

## The Grand Slam Offer (Hormozi Value Equation)

> **Cross-ref:** For complete offer pricing strategy and Value Ladder placement, see `value-ladder.md`. For positioning the offer against competitors, see Business Strategy skill.

### The Value Equation

Hormozi's core framework for constructing irresistible offers:

```
                Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
VALUE = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice
```

**To increase value, you must:**
1. **Increase the Dream Outcome** — Make the promised result bigger, more specific, more desirable
2. **Increase Perceived Likelihood** — Add proof, guarantees, case studies, credentials
3. **Decrease Time Delay** — Promise faster results (and deliver on it)
4. **Decrease Effort and Sacrifice** — Make it easier, done-for-you, require less from them

**The key insight:** Most businesses only compete on #1 (bigger promises) or on price.
The Grand Slam Offer competes on ALL FOUR simultaneously.

### Constructing the Grand Slam Offer — Step by Step

**Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome**

List every problem your customer faces on the way to their desired result.
Don't just list the main problem — list ALL of them, including:
- Problems before they start ("I don't know where to begin")
- Problems during the process ("I'm overwhelmed by the steps")
- Problems after they succeed ("How do I maintain this?")

**Step 2: List All Solutions**

For each problem, brainstorm every possible solution. Think across categories:

| Solution Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Do-it-yourself (education) | Course, guide, book, training |
| Done-with-you (coaching) | Group coaching, live calls, community |
| Done-for-you (service) | Templates, swipe files, setup service |
| Tools and resources | Software, calculators, checklists |
| Access and connection | Mastermind, networking, introductions |
| Speed and convenience | Fast-track option, priority support |

**Step 3: Filter by Delivery Cost and Value**

For each solution, assess:
- **Cost to deliver** (your time, COGS, marginal cost per customer)
- **Perceived value** (how much the customer thinks it's worth)

**Keep:** High perceived value + low delivery cost
**Consider:** High perceived value + medium delivery cost
**Cut:** Low perceived value at any delivery cost

**Step 4: Stack the Offer**

Assemble the components into a stack where each piece addresses a different
problem and a different limiting belief:

```
CORE OFFER: [Main training/product] — addresses Dream Outcome
  + BONUS 1: [Tool/template] — reduces Effort and Sacrifice
  + BONUS 2: [Community/coaching] — increases Perceived Likelihood
  + BONUS 3: [Quick-start guide] — reduces Time Delay
  + BONUS 4: [Case study library] — increases Perceived Likelihood
  + FAST-ACTION BONUS: [1-on-1 call] — decreases Time Delay further
  + GUARANTEE: [Named guarantee] — maximizes Perceived Likelihood
```

**Step 5: Name the Offer Components**

Each component needs a specific, benefit-driven name. Not "Module 3" —
"The Client Acquisition Machine." Not "Bonus PDF" — "The 90-Day Revenue
Roadmap." The name itself should communicate value.

### Guarantee Frameworks

The guarantee is the single most powerful conversion lever after the offer
itself. Types, ranked by aggressiveness:

| Guarantee Type | Structure | Risk Level (for you) | Conversion Lift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unconditional | "Full refund, no questions, keep everything" | Highest | 15-30% lift |
| Conditional | "Do the work → show proof → refund if no results" | Medium | 10-20% lift |
| Anti-guarantee | "No refund. This is for serious people only." | Lowest | Works at high-ticket only |
| Better-than-money-back | "Full refund + $X for your trouble" | Very high | 20-40% lift (if credible) |
| Performance | "You'll get [specific result] or we work free until you do" | Medium | 15-25% lift |
| Hybrid | "Unconditional for 30 days, then conditional for 12 months" | Medium | Best of both |

**The Hormozi Guarantee Stack:**
Combine multiple guarantees for maximum risk reversal:
1. Unconditional 30-day "try it and see" guarantee
2. Conditional 12-month results guarantee (do the work → get the result → or full refund)
3. Name it something memorable: "The Triple-Your-Investment Guarantee"

### Pricing Psychology for Offers

| Technique | How It Works | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Value-to-price gap | State total value ($15,000) before revealing price ($997) | Every webinar, every sales page |
| Payment plans | 3x $397 vs $997 (total is higher but feels smaller) | Orders over $200 |
| Decoy pricing | Three tiers where middle tier is the target | Course/membership offers |
| Price anchoring | Compare to the cost of NOT solving the problem | High-ticket phone closes |
| "Just $X/day" | $997 = "less than $3/day" | Continuity and payment plans |

### Script Length vs Conversion Rate

| Traffic Temperature | Optimal Script Length | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | Long (60-90 min webinar, 5K+ word sales page) | Need full belief-breaking sequence |
| Warm | Medium (30-45 min VSL, 2-4K word page) | Some beliefs already addressed |
| Hot | Short (10-15 min pitch, 500-1K word page) | Trust exists, just need offer details |

**The rule:** Never make copy shorter to "respect people's time." Make it as long
as it needs to be to break all three beliefs and present the full Stack. Short
copy to cold traffic = low conversion. Long copy to hot traffic = fine (they'll
skim to the offer). You can't bore someone into buying, but you can't confuse
them into buying either.
