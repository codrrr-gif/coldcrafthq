## 1. The Core Principle

Every product on your Value Ladder needs its own sales funnel. Different
price points require different funnel architectures, different scripts, and
different levels of trust-building. A free + shipping offer doesn't need a
90-minute webinar. A $25,000 coaching program can't be sold with a
two-minute video. Match the funnel to the rung of the ladder.

---

## Frontend Funnels (Cold/Warm Traffic → Buyers)

### Funnel #1: Two-Step Free + Shipping

**Best for:** Physical products, books, DVDs, free trials
**Price range:** Free + shipping ($0 + $4.95-$9.95)
**Traffic:** Cold and warm

**Page flow:**
```
LANDING PAGE (Who/What/Why/How video + two-step form)
    ↓
STEP 1: Shipping address form (captures email = qualified subscriber)
    ↓
STEP 2: Credit card form (shipping charge = qualified buyer)
    + Order form bump ($37-$97 checkbox offer)
    ↓
OTO PAGE #1 (one-time offer, $97-$297)
    ↓ (yes)              ↓ (no)
OTO PAGE #2 ←──── DOWNSELL PAGE
    ↓
THANK YOU PAGE (+ affiliate recommendations)
```

**Key psychology:**
- Two-step form reduces friction (no credit card visible on step 1)
- Conversion on step 1 is often HIGHER than a regular squeeze page
  because physical product has higher perceived value than digital
- 25% of free + shipping buyers convert on the first upsell
- Average $66 revenue per free book given away (Brunson's data)

**Script for landing page:** Who/What/Why/How (see scripts reference)

### Funnel #2: Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO)

**Best for:** Digital products, online courses, templates
**Price range:** $27-$97
**Traffic:** Cold and warm (via squeeze page)

**Page flow:**
```
SQUEEZE PAGE (free lead magnet to capture email)
    ↓
SLO SALES PAGE (Star, Story, Solution script — video or long-form text)
    + Order form bump ($37-$97)
    ↓
OTO PAGE #1 ($97-$297)
    ↓ (yes)              ↓ (no)
OTO PAGE #2 ←──── DOWNSELL PAGE
    ↓
THANK YOU PAGE
```

**Key psychology:**
- The lead magnet (bribe) gets them to give their email
- The SLO page sells the core frontend product
- Goal: frontend revenue LIQUIDATES ad spend (hence "self-liquidating")
- All upsells become pure profit

**Script for sales page:** Star, Story, Solution (see scripts reference)

### Funnel #3: Continuity Funnel

**Best for:** Memberships, software, subscription services
**Price range:** Free trial / $1 trial / $27-$97 per month
**Traffic:** Best as SECOND funnel (after Soap Opera Sequence bonds with AC)

**Page flow:**
```
LANDING PAGE (Who/What/Why/How for trial OR Star/Story/Solution for paid)
    ↓
ORDER FORM (trial or first month)
    + Order form bump (training on how to use the membership)
    ↓
OTO: Annual/Lifetime option
    ↓
ONBOARDING SEQUENCE (critical for retention)
```

**Key psychology:**
- Continuity works best AFTER the Attractive Character bond is formed
- Using it as a second funnel dramatically increases stick rate
- The order form bump should help them USE the membership (reduces churn)
- Annual/lifetime upsell locks in revenue and reduces cancellation risk

## Middle-of-Ladder Funnels (Warm Traffic → Premium Buyers)

### Funnel #4: The Perfect Webinar

**Best for:** Courses, coaching, programs ($297-$2,997)
**Price range:** $297-$2,997
**Traffic:** Warm traffic (email list, social media followers)

**Page flow:**
```
REGISTRATION PAGE (Who/What/Why/How script)
    ↓
CONFIRMATION PAGE (date, time, instructions)
    ↓
WEBINAR (60-90 minutes: Introduction → Content → The Stack → Close)
    ↓
ORDER PAGE (with payment options)
    ↓
OTO / UPSELL SEQUENCE
```

**For automated (evergreen) webinars, add:**
- Option to watch "yesterday's replay" on confirmation page
- Segmented email sequences based on:
  - Registered but didn't show (sequence to watch replay)
  - Showed but left early (sequence to finish watching)
  - Watched to end but didn't buy (objection-handling sequence)
  - Bought (remove from sales sequence, add to onboarding)

**The webinar structure:**
1. **Introduction (5 min):** Big Promise, Hook to End, Command Attention,
   Qualify Yourself, Future Pace
2. **Content (50-60 min):** The One Thing + Three Secrets that break
   limiting beliefs preventing purchase
3. **The Stack (10-20 min):** Present each offer component individually,
   re-stacking the full offer with each new component. The last thing they
   see is the FULL stack, not the last bonus. Then reveal the price.

**The Stack is the secret weapon:**
- Most presenters: show bonuses in sequence, last thing shown is last bonus
- The Stack: each slide shows the GROWING total offer
- Prospect associates price with the FULL stack, not just the last item
- Brunson went from 5-10% close rate to 20%+ consistently

### Funnel #5: Invisible Funnel Webinar

**Best for:** Building goodwill, ascending warm audience, premium content
**Price range:** $47-$497 (try before you buy)
**Traffic:** Warm and hot traffic

**Page flow:**
```
REGISTRATION PAGE (Magic Bullet script — credit card collected but NOT charged)
    ↓
WEBINAR (3-4 hours of premium content)
    ↓
DECISION POINT:
    - If they love it → do nothing → card charged next day
    - If they don't → email before deadline → no charge
    ↓
SOFT SELL → Application page for high-ticket coaching
```

**Key psychology:**
- "Try before you buy" removes ALL risk from the prospect
- 85% show-up rate (vs. 30% for free webinars) because they have skin in the game
- Only ~10% cancel after (90% pay)
- The 3-4 hours of value creates massive goodwill and Attractive Character bond
- Soft sell at the end to high-ticket = natural ascension, not hard pitch

### Funnel #6: Product Launch Funnel

**Best for:** New product releases, event-driven campaigns
**Price range:** $297-$1,997
**Traffic:** Warm and hot (email list, affiliate lists)

**Page flow:**
```
VIDEO #1: "Wow and How" (big idea + case studies) → hook to Video 2
    ↓ (2-3 days later)
VIDEO #2: "Transformational Education" (walk through the process) → hook to Video 3
    ↓ (2-3 days later)
VIDEO #3: "Ownership Experience" (future pace: what life looks like with this) → hook to Video 4
    ↓ (2-3 days later)
VIDEO #4: "The Offer" (reveal product, price, how to get it)
    ↓
ORDER PAGE → OTO SEQUENCE
```

**Key psychology:**
- Jeff Walker's framework: break the sales presentation into 4 digestible pieces
- Each video provides standalone value while building toward the sale
- Anticipation builds over days (like a movie trailer sequence)
- Works best with affiliate/JV launches where multiple lists mail simultaneously
- Scarcity is natural: launch window opens and closes

## Backend Funnel (Hot Traffic → High-Ticket Clients)

### Funnel #7: High-Ticket Three-Step Application

**Best for:** Coaching, consulting, done-for-you services ($5,000-$100,000+)
**Price range:** $5,000-$100,000+
**Traffic:** Hot traffic (existing buyers, advanced subscribers)

**Page flow:**
```
STEP 1: CASE STUDY PAGE
    Video showing client results + "Apply Now" button
    ↓
STEP 2: APPLICATION FORM
    Detailed questions about their business, goals, budget
    (The application pre-frames: THEY are selling YOU on why you should accept them)
    ↓
STEP 3: HOMEWORK PAGE
    Videos to watch before the call:
    - Video 1: Training on what it takes to succeed
    - Video 2: Emotional backstory (builds AC bond)
    - Video 3: Case studies and testimonials
    + Phone number if they can't wait
    ↓
PHONE CALL (Two-step: Setter → Closer)
```

**Key psychology:**
- Changes the selling environment (online → phone)
- Application flips the dynamic: prospect sells YOU on why they're a good fit
- Homework page pre-frames heavily before the call
- Two-step phone process: setter uncovers emotions/pain, closer provides solution
- Brunson went from 60 salespeople to 2 with this funnel — same revenue

**The two-step phone script:**
- **Setter:** Find the WHY (emotions behind the goal), identify the obstacle
  ("I don't know how"), get them to sell themselves ("If you could work
  with Russell, do you think you'd succeed? Why?")
- **Closer:** Magnify the pain, present the solution, handle objections, close

## Choosing the Right Funnel

| Situation | Recommended Funnel |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Cold traffic, physical product, low price | Two-Step Free + Shipping |
| Cold traffic, digital product, $27-$97 | Self-Liquidating Offer |
| Existing list, monthly revenue needed | Continuity Funnel |
| Existing list, $297-$2,997 product | Perfect Webinar |
| Building goodwill + ascending to high-ticket | Invisible Funnel Webinar |
| New product launch to warm/hot traffic | Product Launch Funnel |
| Selling coaching/consulting $5,000+ | High-Ticket Application |

**The typical company uses multiple funnels:**
```
Cold Traffic → Free + Shipping Funnel → Soap Opera Sequence →
    Continuity Funnel → Perfect Webinar → High-Ticket Application
```

Each funnel feeds the next. Frontend funnels generate buyers. Communication
funnels (Soap Opera + Seinfeld emails) build the AC bond. Middle funnels
ascend buyers. Backend funnels serve the best customers at the highest level.

## Funnel Economics — Benchmarks by Type

These are expected ranges for optimized funnels (after 30-90 days of testing). New funnels
will start below these numbers. Use them as targets, not as day-one expectations.

| Funnel Type | Opt-In Rate | Buyer Conv. | OTO1 Conv. | OTO2 Conv. | Rev/Lead | Break-Even CPL | ROAS (90-day) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free+Shipping** | 30-50% | 8-15% | 20-30% | 10-15% | $3-8 | $3-5 | 2-4x |
| **SLO ($47)** | 25-40% | 2-5% | 15-25% | 8-12% | $2-6 | $2-4 | 1.5-3x |
| **SLO ($97)** | 20-35% | 1-3% | 15-25% | 8-12% | $3-8 | $3-6 | 2-4x |
| **Continuity (trial)** | 20-35% | 5-10% | 15-20% (annual) | — | $2-5 + LTV | $3-5 | 3-8x (12mo) |
| **Perfect Webinar** | 20-35% (reg) | 5-15% of attendees | — | — | $15-80 | $10-30 | 3-8x |
| **Invisible Funnel** | 15-25% (reg) | 85-90% (pay after) | — | — | $30-150 | $20-50 | 4-10x |
| **Product Launch** | — | 3-8% of list | 15-25% | — | $10-50 | $5-20 | 3-10x |
| **High-Ticket App** | 5-15% (apply) | 20-40% of calls | — | — | $200-2,000 | $100-500 | 5-20x |

**Key metric**: Day-1 Revenue Per Lead. If Day-1 RPL > CPL, you have a profitable model.
If not, optimize the funnel before scaling. See `value-ladder.md` Part 6 for the worked calculation.

**Time to profitable by funnel type**:
- Free+Shipping / SLO: 2-4 weeks of testing
- Perfect Webinar: 4-8 weeks (need enough registrants to test)
- Product Launch: Single launch cycle (7-14 days), then iterate
- High-Ticket Application: 4-12 weeks (lower volume, slower feedback)

## Evergreen Webinar Deep Dive

The automated (evergreen) webinar is one of the highest-value funnel types because it
runs 24/7 without you being present. But most evergreen webinars fail because they're
treated as "record once, set and forget." An elite evergreen webinar requires more
sophisticated architecture than a live one.

### Replay Strategy

**Option 1: "Just Missed It" Replay**
- Confirmation page offers: "You just missed yesterday's session — watch the replay now"
- Creates urgency (it already happened) while offering immediate access
- 30-50% of registrants watch the replay vs 25-35% who attend "live"

**Option 2: Scheduled Automated Sessions**
- Offer specific times: "The next session starts in 15 minutes / 1 hour / tomorrow at 2pm"
- Uses countdown timer on the registration page
- Simulates live attendance behavior (they block time, pay more attention)

**Option 3: On-Demand with Deadline**
- "Watch now — this page expires in 48 hours"
- Maximizes convenience but reduces perceived scarcity
- Best for audiences who resist scheduled formats

### Segmentation by Behavior

After registration, segment attendees into 4 buckets and run different email sequences:

| Segment | Behavior | Email Sequence | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Registered, no show** | Signed up but didn't watch | 3-email replay sequence (day 1, 3, 5) | Get them to watch |
| **Watched < 50%** | Started but left early | "You missed the best part" + timestamp link | Get them to finish |
| **Watched > 50%, no buy** | Engaged but didn't convert | 5-7 email objection-handling sequence | Overcome specific objections |
| **Bought** | Converted | Remove from sales sequence → onboarding | Deliver value, reduce refunds |

> *See `communication-funnels.md` for the Soap Opera and Seinfeld email frameworks
> that power these sequences. See the Elite Copywriting skill's `email-copy.md` for
> email copy craft.*

### Urgency Mechanisms for Evergreen

Live webinars have natural urgency (it's happening now). Evergreen needs manufactured urgency:

- **Expiring replay**: "This replay comes down in 48 hours" (use Deadline Funnel or similar)
- **Expiring bonus**: "Order in the next 24 hours and get [bonus]" (unique per registrant)
- **Price increase**: "The special webinar price expires at midnight" (cookie-based timer)
- **Limited spots**: "Only 20 spots left in this cohort" (if genuinely capped)

**Critical rule**: The urgency must be REAL. Fake countdown timers that reset on refresh
destroy trust and get you banned from ad platforms. Use tools that enforce genuine deadlines
per registrant (Deadline Funnel, ThriveCart countdown, custom cookie logic).

### Evergreen Optimization Loop

```
Week 1-2: Run live webinar 2-3 times → find the version that converts best
Week 3:   Record the best version → set up automated delivery
Week 4-6: Test replay strategy (just-missed vs scheduled vs on-demand)
Week 6-8: Build and test the 4 segmented email sequences
Week 8+:  Optimize headlines, registration page, email subject lines
          Test new hooks monthly (ads fatigue, webinar content stays)
```

## Common Mistakes by Funnel Type

### Free+Shipping
| Mistake | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Shipping cost too high | Conversion drops below 5% | Keep shipping under $7.95; subsidize if needed |
| No order form bump | Leaving 30-40% revenue on the table | Add a relevant $37-$97 checkbox offer |
| Lead magnet attracts freebie seekers | Low upsell conversion, high refunds | Qualify harder in the bait (specific to dream customer) |

### SLO
| Mistake | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Sales page too short for price | Low conversion on $97 offers | $97 needs 2,000-5,000 words or 15-30 min VSL |
| No squeeze page before SLO | Losing the email for non-buyers | Always capture email FIRST, then sell |
| Weak guarantee | High cart abandonment | Stronger guarantee (60-90 day, keep the product) |

### Perfect Webinar
| Mistake | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Too much content, not enough belief-breaking | High engagement but low close rate | Each Secret must break a LIMITING BELIEF, not just teach |
| No Stack slide | Close rate under 5% | Build the Stack — show the growing offer visually |
| No show-up sequence | 15-20% show rate (should be 30%+) | 3-email reminder sequence: 24hr, 1hr, "we're live" |

### High-Ticket Application
| Mistake | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Application too short | Unqualified calls, low close rate | 15-20 questions minimum; longer app = more committed applicants |
| No homework page | Prospect arrives cold to the call | Add 3 videos: training, backstory, case studies |
| Setter and closer are the same person | Emotional discovery gets skipped | Separate roles: setter uncovers pain, closer presents solution |
