## The Core Principle

The business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins.
— Dan Kennedy (via Brunson)

This is the single most important idea in funnel architecture. Every funnel
design decision traces back to this: How do we maximize the revenue per
customer so we can outspend every competitor on acquisition?

The answer is NOT "sell one thing at one price." The answer is a VALUE LADDER
— a sequence of ascending offers that takes a customer from first contact to
highest-level service, increasing both the value delivered AND the price charged
at each step.

## Part 1: The Secret Formula (Brunson)

Before building any funnel, answer four questions:

**1. WHO is your dream customer?**
Not demographics — psychographics. What do they care about? What are they
passionate about? What are their goals, dreams, and desires? Create a vivid
avatar with a name and picture. (See Research & Market Analysis skill for the
full avatar profiling process.)

**2. WHERE can you find them?**
Where do they congregate online? What Facebook groups, subreddits, forums,
newsletters, blogs, YouTube channels, podcasts? Brunson calls these
"congregations" — the online equivalent of churches where people with
similar beliefs gather. Traffic is about finding congregations, not creating them.

**3. What BAIT will you use to attract them?**
Your bait is the first touchpoint — a free or low-cost offer that attracts your
dream customer and repels everyone else. The bait must match what your
dream customer values. If your bait attracts the wrong people, your entire
funnel breaks.

**4. What RESULT do you want to give them?**
Not what product you sell — what transformation you deliver. The result is the
highest level of your Value Ladder. If you could help your dream customer
achieve their ultimate goal, what would that look like?

## Part 2: The Value Ladder

### Structure

```
                VALUE
                  ↑
                  |        $$$$$  [Backend: High-Ticket Service]
                  |       $$$$   [Premium Offer]
                  |      $$$    [Core Offer]
                  |     $$     [Entry Offer]
                  |    $      [Bait / Lead Magnet]
                  |___________________________→ PRICE
```

Each rung of the ladder:
- Provides MORE value than the previous rung
- Charges MORE money than the previous rung
- Is a natural progression from the previous rung

### The Ladder Rungs

**Rung 1: Bait (Free or Free + Shipping)**
- Purpose: Attract dream customers, qualify buyers
- Price: Free, or free + shipping ($0-$9.95)
- Examples: Free book, free report, free tool, free DVD, free trial
- Key insight: The power of FREE is irrational. 8% of cold traffic converts
  on free + shipping vs. 1% on a $197 offer (Brunson's 100-Visitor Test)
- Funnel type: Two-Step Free + Shipping Funnel or Squeeze Page → SLO Funnel
  (see `funnel-types.md`, Funnels #1 and #2)

**Rung 2: Frontend / Entry Offer ($7-$97)**
- Purpose: Convert subscribers to buyers, cover ad costs
- Price: Low enough to be a no-brainer
- Examples: Book, mini-course, template pack, tool, trial
- Key insight: "A buyer is a buyer is a buyer" (Dan Kennedy). Once someone
  pulls out their credit card, they're 10x more likely to buy again.
- Funnel type: Self-Liquidating Offer Funnel (see `funnel-types.md`, Funnel #2)
- Copy approach: Star, Story, Solution script (see `scripts-and-frameworks.md`)

**Rung 3: Core Offer ($97-$997)**
- Purpose: Deliver the main solution, generate profit
- Price: Matched to the value of the outcome
- Examples: Full course, coaching program, software, service package
- Key insight: This is where most businesses start and stop. Without the
  lower rungs, you can't afford to acquire customers. Without the higher
  rungs, you leave money on the table.
- Funnel type: Perfect Webinar or Product Launch Funnel
  (see `funnel-types.md`, Funnels #4 and #6)
- Copy approach: Perfect Webinar script or long-form sales page
  (see Elite Copywriting skill's `sales-pages.md`)

**Rung 4: Premium / High-Ticket ($997-$25,000+)**
- Purpose: Serve your best customers at the highest level
- Price: Premium, justified by personalized service
- Examples: Mastermind, done-for-you service, private coaching, events
- Key insight: A percentage of customers WANT to pay more for more value.
  If you don't offer it, they'll find someone else who will.
- Funnel type: High-Ticket Application Funnel (see `funnel-types.md`, Funnel #7)
- Copy approach: Case study video + application page
  (see `scripts-and-frameworks.md` for setter/closer scripts)

**Rung 5: Continuity (Recurring)**
- Purpose: Predictable revenue, ongoing relationship
- Price: Monthly/annual subscription
- Examples: Membership, software, subscription box, retainer
- Key insight: "If you don't have continuity, you don't have a business"
  (David Frey via Brunson). Recurring revenue is the lifeblood.
- Funnel type: Continuity Funnel (see `funnel-types.md`, Funnel #3)
- Copy approach: Who/What/Why/How for trial, Star/Story/Solution for paid
  (see `scripts-and-frameworks.md`)

### Building Your Value Ladder

**If you only have a core offer:**
- Add a frontend bait (what can you give away for free that attracts dream customers?)
- Add a backend premium (what would you do if you could serve them at the highest level?)
- Add continuity (what can you charge monthly for ongoing value?)

**If you only have a frontend:**
- What additional value can you provide? (Coaching, implementation, community)
- Can you be more hands-on? (DFY > DWY > DIY)
- What ancillary products enhance the core? (Tools, templates, training)

**The fractal rule:** There is no top to the Value Ladder. If 20% of your
$1,000 customers would pay $5,000 for more value, and 20% of THOSE would
pay $25,000, the ladder keeps going. (Marshall's 80/20 fractal principle.)

## Part 3: The Money Model (Hormozi)

### Maximizing Day-1 Revenue

Hormozi's Money Model framework extends the Value Ladder by focusing on
IMMEDIATE revenue per customer:

**Attraction Offer** → Gets them in the door (often at break-even or loss)
**Core Offer** → The main product/service
**Upsell #1** → If yes to core → offer more value
**Downsell #1** → If no to core → offer less (save the sale)
**Upsell #2** → If yes to upsell #1 → offer even more
**Downsell #2** → If no to upsell #1 → offer alternative
**Continuity** → Recurring revenue

```
ATTRACTION OFFER
    ↓
CORE OFFER
    ↓ (yes)              ↓ (no)
UPSELL #1 ←──────── DOWNSELL #1
    ↓ (yes)              ↓ (no)
UPSELL #2 ←──────── DOWNSELL #2
    ↓
CONTINUITY
```

**The test:** Calculate Day-1 Revenue Per Lead.
If Day-1 Revenue Per Lead > Cost Per Lead → profitable Money Model.
If Day-1 Revenue Per Lead < Cost Per Lead → fix the model before scaling.

### Upsell Rules (Brunson + Hormozi)

**Rule #1: Don't sell more of the same thing.**
If they bought a juicing ebook, don't upsell another juicing product. That
itch is scratched. Sell the NEXT thing they need (workout plan, supplements).

**Rule #2: Don't sell something random.**
The upsell must be logically connected to the initial purchase.

**Three upsell structures:**
1. **The Next Thing** — What do they need next to reach their goal?
2. **Do It Faster** — A tool or shortcut that accelerates results
3. **Need Help?** — Done-with-you or done-for-you implementation

### Order Form Bumps

Adding a simple checkbox offer on the order form (AFTER credit card info,
BEFORE submit button) converts at 30-40%. This is the highest-leverage
addition to any funnel. Two lines of text and a checkbox can increase
frontend revenue by 30-40% with zero additional traffic.

## Part 4: Frontend vs. Backend Strategy

### Frontend Funnels (Cold/Warm Traffic)

Purpose: Acquire customers. Break even or accept small losses.
Traffic: Cold and warm traffic
Products: Low-cost, high-perceived-value
Goal: Get buyers into the Value Ladder

Frontend funnels use simpler, shorter scripts because the price is low and
the decision is easy. The goal is NOT profit — it's customer acquisition.
(See `funnel-types.md` Funnels #1-#3 for the specific page flows.)

### Middle Funnels (Warm Traffic)

Purpose: Ascend customers up the Value Ladder
Traffic: Existing subscribers and buyers
Products: Mid-range ($297-$2,000)
Goal: Deliver core transformation, generate profit

Middle funnels use longer scripts (webinars, product launches) because the
price requires more education and trust-building. The Soap Opera Sequence
and Seinfeld emails (see `communication-funnels.md`) bridge the gap between
frontend purchase and middle-funnel offer.

### Backend Funnels (Hot Traffic)

Purpose: Serve best customers at highest level
Traffic: Existing buyers with proven engagement
Products: High-ticket ($5,000-$100,000+)
Goal: Maximum value delivery, maximum revenue

Backend funnels usually require changing the selling environment — phone
calls, applications, live events. Most people won't click a $25,000 buy button
on a webpage. But they WILL fill out an application and get on a call.
(See `funnel-types.md` Funnel #7 for the three-step application blueprint.)

## Part 7: Pricing Psychology

### Price Anchoring (The Stack)

Price anchoring is the most powerful pricing tool in the funnel builder's
arsenal. Brunson's "Stack" technique is price anchoring applied systematically.

**How it works:**
1. Present each component of your offer individually with its own value
2. Show the running total as you add each component
3. The total stack value becomes the anchor price
4. Reveal the actual price AFTER the anchor is established
5. The gap between anchor and actual price = perceived deal

**Worked example — $497 course "The Stack":**

| Component | Individual Value |
|-----------|-----------------|
| 12-Week Video Training (52 lessons) | $1,997 |
| Private Community Access (lifetime) | $497 |
| Weekly Q&A Call Recordings Library | $997 |
| Bonus: Done-for-You Templates | $297 |
| Bonus: Case Study Breakdown Videos | $497 |
| Bonus: Quick-Start Implementation Guide | $197 |
| **Total Stack Value** | **$4,482** |
| **Your Price Today** | **$497** |

The prospect doesn't evaluate $497 in isolation — they evaluate it against
the $4,482 anchor. At 11% of the "real" value, the decision shifts from
"is this worth $497?" to "can I really get $4,482 of value for $497?"

**Rules for effective stacking:**
- Each component must have a believable standalone value
- The total must be at least 5-10x the actual price (Brunson's guideline)
- Present the stack visually — build it on screen, slide by slide
- The LAST slide before the price reveal shows the FULL stack, not the last bonus
- Never show the price until the full anchor is established

> **Cross-reference:** See `scripts-and-frameworks.md` for the Perfect
> Webinar Stack sequence. See Elite Copywriting skill's `sales-pages.md`
> for how to write each stack element with benefit-driven descriptions.

### Payment Plan Strategy

Payment plans increase conversions but reduce total collected revenue. Use
them strategically.

**When to offer payment plans:**
- Price point above $297 (below this, payment plans create more support
  overhead than they're worth)
- Products with delayed results (coaching, courses) where the buyer needs
  time to see ROI
- When your funnel economics need higher conversion rates more than they
  need higher per-sale revenue

**Pricing the payment plan (the premium principle):**

A payment plan should ALWAYS cost MORE than pay-in-full. This is not
punitive — it compensates for the risk of default and delayed cash flow.

| Pay-In-Full Price | Payment Plan | Premium |
|-------------------|-------------|---------|
| $497 | 3 × $197 = $591 | 19% |
| $997 | 4 × $297 = $1,188 | 19% |
| $2,997 | 6 × $597 = $3,582 | 20% |
| $10,000 | 12 × $997 = $11,964 | 20% |

**Benchmark:** 18-22% premium is standard. Below 15% and you're giving away
too much. Above 25% and payment plan buyers feel punished.

**Expected split:** 40-50% will choose pay-in-full, 50-60% will choose
the payment plan. Payment plan default rates average 15-25% (meaning
15-25% of payment plan buyers stop paying before completing all installments).

**Net revenue calculation:**
```
100 buyers at $997 pay-in-full option:
  50 pay-in-full:  50 × $997 = $49,850
  50 payment plan: 50 × $1,188 = $59,400 (gross)
                   minus 20% default: $59,400 × 0.80 = $47,520 (collected)
  Total collected: $97,370
  vs. 100 pay-in-full only: $99,700
  vs. conversion lift: payment plans typically increase conversion 20-30%
```

If adding a payment plan lifts your conversion rate by 25% (from 100 buyers
to 125 buyers), you collect MORE total revenue even accounting for defaults.
Run the numbers for your specific funnel.

### Trial-to-Paid Conversion Benchmarks

For continuity/subscription offers (Rung 5), the trial structure matters:

| Trial Type | Trial-to-Paid Rate | Notes |
|------------|-------------------|-------|
| Free trial (no CC required) | 3-8% | Highest volume, lowest conversion |
| Free trial (CC required) | 25-40% | Lower sign-ups but much higher conversion |
| $1 trial (7 days) | 50-65% | Best balance of volume and conversion |
| $1 trial (14 days) | 45-55% | Slightly lower conversion, more time to evaluate |
| Paid trial ($7-$29) | 65-80% | Lowest volume, highest conversion and retention |

**Key insight:** CC-required free trials and $1 trials are the sweet spot
for most info-product and SaaS businesses. The $1 charge creates a
micro-commitment that dramatically improves conversion AND long-term
retention (buyers who pay even $1 have 3-5x higher 90-day retention than
free trial users).

### The Decoy Effect Applied to Offer Tiers

The decoy effect (asymmetric dominance) is when introducing a third option
makes one of the original two options dramatically more attractive.

**Classic application — SaaS pricing page:**

| Plan | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Basic | $29/mo | 5 users, core features |
| Professional (THE DECOY) | $79/mo | 10 users, core + advanced features |
| Business | $99/mo | Unlimited users, all features, priority support |

Professional exists primarily to make Business look like a no-brainer. For
only $20/mo more than Professional, you get unlimited users and priority
support. Without the Professional tier, customers compare Basic ($29) to
Business ($99) — a 3.4x price jump that feels steep. With the decoy,
customers compare Professional ($79) to Business ($99) — a 1.25x jump
that feels trivial.

**How to engineer decoys:**
1. Identify your target tier (the one you WANT most buyers to choose)
2. Create a decoy that is similar to the target but worse on one key dimension
3. Price the decoy close to (but below) the target tier
4. Make the value gap between decoy and target obviously larger than the price gap

**Decoy effect benchmarks:** A well-designed decoy shifts 20-30% of buyers
from the lower tier to the target tier. On a 1,000-customer base, shifting
25% from $29 Basic to $99 Business adds $21,000/month in recurring revenue.

**Application beyond SaaS — course pricing:**

| Option | Price | What's Included |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| DIY (course only) | $497 | Video modules, templates |
| DWY (DECOY) | $1,497 | Course + 4 group calls + community |
| DFY (target) | $1,997 | Course + 12 group calls + community + 2 private calls + done-for-you templates |

The DWY tier makes the DFY tier look like the obvious choice — for just $500
more, you get 3x the calls, private coaching, and custom templates.
